Typical sharing scenarios

Sharing with a colleague Inside your organisation:
  • Use the Share button on the file.
  • Type their name and select it when it appears.
  • Choose whether they can Edit or just View.
  • This is the quickest and safest way to give someone access to a single file.
Sharing with someone Outside your organisation (when allowed):
  • Follow your organisation‚Äôs guest access rules ‚Äî many require adding the person as a guest first.
  • Share only the specific site or folder they truly need.
  • Use link expiry dates or view-only links when working with sensitive information.
  • Guests sign in with their own account; no password sharing is needed.

When do you need to adjust permissions?

  • When a teammate joins your project and needs access to shared files or a specific site.
  • When someone leaves the project and should no longer see or edit certain content.
  • When working with sensitive documents where only a small group should have edit rights.
  • When sharing with external partners and access must be limited or temporary.
  • When a library grows and different groups need different levels of access to folders.

Understanding when to share a file vs. when to change site permissions will help you work more confidently and avoid oversharing.
