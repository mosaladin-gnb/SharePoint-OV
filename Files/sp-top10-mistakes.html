Top 10 SharePoint mistakes to avoid

Here are the most common pitfalls people run into when working in SharePoint, along with easier, cleaner ways to handle each situation. Use this as a quick guide to keep your sites organised and frustration-free.

Common pitfalls & better alternatives

These examples highlight habits that often cause clutter, confusion, or extra work. The alternative suggestions are simple improvements you can start using right away.

1
Using folders for everything
Folders can get deep and messy quickly, making files harder to find and harder to manage.
Better:
Use a few simple folders and let columns, filters, and views do the heavy lifting.
2
Dumping files in one giant library
Putting all files into one place makes it hard to organise or tell what belongs where.
Better:
Create separate libraries for different types of content so each area stays tidy

(Projects, Templates, Reference, etc.).
3
Breaking inheritance everywhere
Giving unique permissions to lots of files and folders makes access difficult to track.
Better:
Keep permissions simple at the site or library level. Use individual sharing only when necessary.
4
Guests added without a plan
Random guest access creates risk and confusion, especially when guests leave.
Better:
Use dedicated external-facing sites and review guest access regularly.
5
Ignoring version history
People often create multiple copies or accidentally overwrite content.
Better:
Use version history to undo mistakes and keep a clean, single source of truth.
6
No home page or navigation
If users land in a random library, they may not understand the purpose of the site.
Better:
Create a simple home page and keep the navigation clear so the site feels intentional.
